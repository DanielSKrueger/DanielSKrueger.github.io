Luminex Electrical and
Systems Engineer 2021

The first project I started working on when I joined Luminex was the integration of a single board computer (SBC) with an imaging instrument. The SBC was intended to control the imaging PCB of the instrument and record data collected by the imaging PCBA. The instrument would perform the necessary amplification and digitalization of the imaging signals. Our objective was to synchronize the two platforms and ensure that the system had sufficient power.

Another project I was assigned to was analyzing the noise of an analog amplification system. The system was designed to amplify the signal from an optical sensor and input it into an ADC. The board consisted of several operational amplifiers connected to an ADC. Our goal was to assess the noise of each amplification stage and compare the overall error to the accuracy of the ADC (Analog to digital converter). Our team concluded that the circuit has a low enough noise magnitude that the ADC would not pick up the noise.

Capstone Senior Design
Team Champion

Our team designed, tested, and implemented a wireless trimodal neural interface device capable of electrical neural recording, electrochemical neural recording, and optogenetic stimulation. Neural interface devices are used to gather information on how neurons communicate with one another and are vital to treating neurological disorders like Parkinsonâ€™s disease and depression. Currently, neural interfaces are targeted for use on freely moving animals and therefore require wireless transmission of data. These devices also utilize optogenetic (or light) stimulation to probe neural tissues and measure the resulting response. Neurons use both electrical and electrochemical signals to communicate with each other, however previous neural interfaces fail to provide both electrical and electrochemical recording. Therefore, our team designed a device that implements in vivo detection (on tissue internal to the animal subject) and includes adjustable optogenetic stimulation, electrical neural recording, and electrochemical neural recording to achieve a clearer image on neural communication pathways in the brain.

Amplifier Design Champion

I won a design competition for a transistor low power amplifier. The amplifier has an input impedance of greater than 480 kohms and an output impedance of less than 558 ohms. My design won because I consumed the least power to meet the criteria. This design only uses a current of 239 micro-amps.
